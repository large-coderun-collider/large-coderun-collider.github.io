About

LCRC is a personal engineering and art project: I study concepts by implementing small, testable artifacts and writing short notes about what I learned.

The CERN framing is a literary device. You’ll find small easter eggs and playful naming throughout the site. Still, the real goal is practical clarity.

What I can’t build, I don’t understand.

Most fills are written in Python (fast feedback, rich ecosystem). Other languages appear when they add a useful angle.

Fill structure

  • Accelerator: a short introduction and the problem statement.
  • MAPS — Model / Architecture / Patterns / Structure: what we’re building and why.
  • BOB — Building on Basics: constraints, invariants, trade-offs, references.
  • CMS — Compact Modeling System: the implementation (language is a tool, not a religion).
  • LCRCb — beauty detector: tests, edge cases, failure modes, pitfalls, small improvements.

Scope

Fills can be “mini systems” or just algorithms. If it’s small, testable, and teaches something real, it belongs here.

Repositories

This site is the narrative. The code lives in the experiments repository.
